    Character creation¶

    This section outlines the steps necessary to create a character from scratch while creating an example character. It assumes that you are somehow familiar with either the Q* rules or P&P RPG Systems in general. For detailed rules see the corresponding sections.

    At character creation, and while gameplay, each improvement costs Downtime. The Downtime costs are listed in the corresponding sections, e.g. Abilities and Skills. Improvements while gameplay can also require additional circumstances, e.g. upgrading Might could cost Credits to cybernatically enhance the character.

    Every creature starts with an amount of Downtime that can be used to improve your character. Decreasing your character’s power through lowering ranks or buying Flaws nets you Downtime, but only to an extent. E.g. a GM can restrict you in how far you can lower an Ability or a Skill.

    During character creation, the global maximum for Downtime can exceed 10.

    The Downtime received for character creation is determined by the creature’s Race and Flaws.

    Also, while creating a character Downtime can be traded for any other Means. One Downtime provides two of any other Means, or one of two Currencies each. E.g. a rich businessman could trade some of his Downtime for Resources or Influence

    Example character idea

    In this example we use the scifi Q* setting. Our character is a human bio-engineer, who has been trained for off-world operations. As such, he knows how to handle light weapons. He has a lot of connections from his earlier life at the research institute he was working at.

    The following sub-sections show how this idea translates into the Q* - RPG System.

    Buying / Reducing Downtime¶

    Downtime cost can be reduced for improvements by spending Credits. The Downtime cost can never be lower than 1.

    In addition to Credits cost, lowering the Downtime can also require additional circumstances, such as trainers, shops, items, etc.

    Downtime reduced Cost [Cr.]
    1 5 000
    2 20 000
    3 45 000
    4 80 000
    5 125 000

    Downtime reduction formular

    (Downtime ^ 2) * 5 000

    Abilities¶

    Every creature starts with its Ability ranks set to Ability - Poor, unless otherwise noted by its Race and Flaws.

    Skills¶

    Every creature starts with its Skill ranks set to Skill - Poor, unless otherwise noted by its Race and Flaws.

    Additionally, Skill ranks can never be higher than their corresponding Ability ranks.

    Example

    A character with Might - Good can never have a might skill at rank Skill - Great or better.
