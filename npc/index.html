    Non-Player Characters¶

    Most characters the GM is playing and enemies are Non-Player Characters (NPCs). NPCs reduce the rule overhead to make it easier for the GM to manage multiple characters at once.

    A GM can however create normal characters in place of NPCs to flesh them out more.

    Difficulty Level¶

    NPCs don’t use Downtime. Instead an NPC has a Difficulty Level that describes how well trained, tough and strong it is.

    Abilities & Skills¶

    An NPC can make all Ability or Skill checks with its Difficulty Level divided by 2 as a modifier.

    Ability and Skill checks

    3d6 + Difficulty Level / 2 + Circumstance

    Specialized NPCs¶

    Specialized NPCs receive their full Difficulty Level on a set amount of Skills. All other skills do not gain a bonus from Difficulty Level. The formular below shows how many specialized Skills a specialized NPC has.

    Specialized NPCs and Skills

    1 + Difficulty Level / 3 + Circumstance

    Abilities are not effected by this.

    Defence¶

    NPCs don’t have to roll for defending.

    NPC Defence

    8 + Difficulty Level + Circumstance

    Pools¶

    Health (HP)¶

    Maximum Health

    Difficulty Level x5 ± Circumstance

    Endurance (EP)¶

    Maximum Endurance

    Difficulty Level x10 ± Circumstance

    Damage¶

    An NPC can use weapons normally, dealing their respective END and DMG values as damage.

    Simplified Health damage

    (1 + Difficulty Level) x 2

    Simplified Endurance damage

    (1 + Difficulty Level) x 5

    Loot¶

    An NPC can drop items, but doesn’t have to. E.g. animals do not drop items, but can be butchered for food, or skinned to make a profit later.

    Credits only make a small portion of the loot, if any at all. The most valuable kind of loot are Resources.

    Credits¶

    If an NPC can drop Credits, they can also have a Wealth descriptor. The Wealth of an NPC adjusts the Credits that can be looted.

    Credits loot

    50 x (2 + Difficulty Level) x (0.5 or 1 or 2)

    Items¶

    Items an NPC was using can also be looted. Depending on the combat, most items have a bad Equipment Condition though.

    Equipment Condition¶

    Stronger NPCs with a higher Difficulty Level use better items.
